I have tried many different research directions and am still exploring my favorite ones.

I. I started with studying large scale "similarity search" problem in my second year with Prof. James Cheng where I learned advanced data structures like kd-tree, skip list, index etc and important big data algorithms, e.g., map-reduce, locality sensitive hashing, quantizaion and K-nearest graph. In addition to theories, I was trained to understand software systems (e.g., Faiss) and master various tools (e.g., cmake and HDFS).

II. During my exchange period in ETH Zurich, apart from enjoying the picturesque Switzerland, I helped Prof. Ce Zhang and Kaan Kara on their new stochastic gradient descend algorithm on database systems. I augmented their system to enable more efficient training, hyper-parameter tuning and profiling and most importantly, helped them understand the trade-offs between statistic efficiency and system efficiency.

III. During the summer break in ETH, I flied to US and attended the summer research internship at Carnegie Mellon University. This internship is fully sponsored by the Charles K.Kao research scholarship. I, together with Prof. Pulkit Grover's group, worked on personalization problem in machine learning. We studied how personalization can be properly achieved in face of "rabbit holes", bubbles that isolate people from different voices. We proposed a novel recommendation system based on our findings.

IV. The most recent one is my thesis in OLAP graph database. I learned classical and popular graph computation and mining systems (e.g., Pregel, G-Miner and Arabseque) and understood different graph processing paradigms (e.g., vertex-centric vs. edge-centric vs. subgraph-centric, synchronous vs. asynchronous). I understood in-depth how existing commercial graph databases lack in more native data storage and adaptive parallelism. I am now working on improving an asynchronous, actor-model based graph processing and storage system, Grasper.
